The 3 Pillars of Scrum (Empiricism)

These principles ensure that all decisions are based on observable facts, experience, and evidence. 

- **Transparency:** Significant aspects of the process must be visible to those responsible for the outcome. This requires a common standard and shared understanding of progress across the entire Scrum Team. ^[[1](https://www.acronymat.com/2021/02/22/scrum-pillars/)] 
- **Inspection:** The Scrum artifacts (e.g., Product Backlog, Sprint Backlog, Increment) and progress toward agreed-upon goals must be inspected frequently and diligently to detect undesirable variances. ^[[1](https://www.acronymat.com/2021/02/22/scrum-pillars/), [2](https://instituteprojectmanagement.com/blog/what-is-scrum/)]
- **Adaptation:** If inspection reveals that aspects of a process deviate outside acceptable limits or that the product is unacceptable, the process or the material being processed must be adjusted as soon as possible. ^[[1](https://www.acronymat.com/2021/02/22/scrum-pillars/), [2](https://scrumguides.org/scrum-guide.html), [3](https://thinklouder.com/answers/3-pillars-5-values-scrum/), [4](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QSiKv_umS-0&t=33)]

The 4 Pillars (Values) of Agile

If you are looking for the four foundational tenets of the overarching Agile framework that Scrum operates within, they are: ^[[1](https://www.wrike.com/agile-guide/faq/what-are-pillars-of-agile/)]

- **Individuals and Interactions** over processes and tools.
- **Working Software** (or deliverables) over comprehensive documentation.
- **Customer Collaboration** over contract negotiation.
- **Responding to Change** over following a strict plan. ^[[1](https://www.wrike.com/agile-guide/faq/what-are-pillars-of-agile/)]
